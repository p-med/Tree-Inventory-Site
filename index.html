Tree Inventory and Carbon Assessment

This website allows you to register a tree, its location, age and other measurements like DBH (diameter at the breast height level) and height. This information will help to create a crowdsourced forest inventory and also will tell you how much CO2 has your tree captured from the environment. The result is returned in kg of CO2.

The carbon storage is calculated with this EPA provided formula:



Carbon storage = π * DBH 2 * h *⁢ WD *⁢ CF 4

Where:


  • DBH (Diameter at Breast Height): Diameter in centimeters of the tree trunk measured at approximately 1.3 meters (4.5 feet) above ground level.
  • H (Height): Total height of the tree measured in meters.
  • WD (Wood Density): Measured in kg/m³, varies by tree species. Mass of wood per unit volume.
  • CF (Carbon Fraction): Standard value representing the proportion of carbon in wood. It is assumed that about 50% of the dry weight of wood is carbon, so the carbon fraction is 0.5.
